About Me

I am a first year Ph.D. student in Algorithms, Combinatorics, and Optimization (ACO) at the Georgia Institute of Technology where I am fortunate to be advised by Dr. Rachel Cummings. My research interests include studying the algorithmic foundations of privacy, in particular the relationship between the local and trusted curator models of differential privacy. I am also interested in studying privacy as it relates to economics and, more broadly, the social sciences.

I received my B.S. in Computer Science and B.S. in Operations Research both from the Georgia Institute of Technology in May 2018. I spent the summer before graduate school studying mean estimation algorithms in the hybrid model of differential privacy with Dr. Aleksandra Korolova as a Visiting Research Scholar at USC.
